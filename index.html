The Estate

A Garden That Breathes

Borokai's tea garden thrives on minimalist intervention. The intense local monsoon climate, the deep alluvial silt carried from the Brahmaputra, and the shade of ancient forest trees dictate the character of every leaf - not chemical intervention or industrial machinery.


Our garden is a habitat. Wild animals move through it freely. Bees pollinate without disturbance. The soil is a living culture nurtured by over a century of forest composting. This is not farming - it is stewardship.

Quiet Luxury

A Monastic Peace in Every Sip

Inspired by the meditative traditions of the Brahmaputra valley, our production ethos is one of deliberate restraint. We believe true luxury is not abundance - it is the absence of compromise. By keeping our yields intentionally small, we guarantee that every canister leaving this estate carries a profound depth of flavor that mass production can never replicate.


Pour Borokai slowly. Breathe deeply. This tea asks for stillness.

A Living Inheritance

Same Roots Planted in 1860

Every single leaf harvested at Borokai comes from the exact same ancient plants that were first planted in these terraces in 1860. Unlike modern tea estates that constantly uproot and replant with high-yielding cloned cultivars, we preserve our heritage plants. Their roots run exceptionally deep into the nutrient-dense iron-rich Assam clay, drawing complex characters and carrying a living biological memory that is more than 165 years old.

Natural Biodiversity

Sanctuary of Jugnus & Rare Butterflies

Our commitment to 0% chemical intervention has turned Borokai into an ecological sanctuary. When night falls, our estate is illuminated by thousands of jugnus (fireflies) - a magical sight now sadly lost to the rest of India due to pesticide usage and light pollution. During the day, rare butterflies flock to the garden, sensitive creatures that only thrive in places of absolute chemical purity.

Purity Tested

Laboratory-Pure Organic Cleanliness

When we say organic, we mean absolute purity. Every harvest is tested to ensure zero chemical residue, heavy metals, or toxic additives. We nourish our soil with natural forest compost and local river sediment, creating a closed-loop system of absolute cleanliness. This is tea in its most pristine form - honest, purifying, and chemical-free.

Tranquil Coexistence

The Free-Roaming Horses of Borokai

A walk through our estate reveals another unique aspect of Borokai's living sanctuary: our free-roaming horses. These beautiful, gentle creatures graze peacefully among the tea terraces, their presence a living testament to the undisturbed serenity of the estate. Here, nature, wildlife, and slow human craft exist in complete, quiet harmony.

Wild Neighbors

Jackals & Leopards of the Estate

Our tea terraces are part of a larger ecosystem, sharing the land with native wildlife such as jackals and leopards. This coexistence is respected and preserved, making Borokai a true haven where the natural world thrives alongside our craft.

Estate Stewardship

Custodians of Borokai

Rajen Sudan

A Lifetime of Compassion

The modern era of Borokai belongs to Rajen Sudan, a man defined not by commerce, but by an extraordinary love for living things. Since childhood, Rajen fed strays, rehabilitated injured animals, and treated the jungle around Borokai as sacred.

His greatest story is the two leopard cubs found orphaned at the edge of the estate. He bottle-fed them in his home and guided their return to the wild. This relationship with the forest is the philosophy behind our tea. We grow in harmony with wildlife, refuse chemicals because the soil is alive, and keep yields small because the land must rest.

His reverence for nature extends to every corner of the estate. At Rajen's request, no flowers are plucked at Borokai. He believes every flower and leaf belongs first to the bees, butterflies, and countless other creatures that depend upon them.

Rajen's compassion for animals and nature is not a philosophy separate from his tea - It is the tea.

The Seed to Packaging Journey

The Craft of Patience

From ancestral Assamica seeds in native soil to the wax-sealed canister on your shelf - eight stages of unhurried devotion.

01 Stage One

The Seed Selection

Our cycle begins with seed collection from ancestral wild-growing Camellia sinensis assamica bushes at the estate boundaries. Seeds are hand-inspected for weight and integrity - only the most resilient carry forward the heritage lineage of Borokai to the next generation of bushes.

02 Stage Two

The Nursery Phase

Sown in richly composted organic beds, the delicate seedlings spend 12 to 18 months inside shaded bamboo shelters. Master horticulturists monitor moisture levels daily, shielding young roots from the fierce tropical wind and teaching each plant to be resilient before it ever touches open ground.

03 Stage Three

Field Transplanting

Strong saplings are hand-transplanted into Borokai's alluvial terraces, positioned to catch natural hillside runoff and benefit from the shade canopy of ancient forest trees. No irrigation systems, no fertilizer lines - just rain, soil, and the accumulated wisdom of generations.

04 Stage Four

Fine Hand Plucking

At first light, when the morning mist still clings to the garden, our master pluckers move through the rows selecting only the classic two leaves and a bud. This is learned knowledge - the fingers know which shoot is ready, which must be left. Harvesting is an act of listening.

05 Stage Five

Ambient Withering

Harvested leaves are spread immediately onto elevated bamboo shelves, exposed to slow cross-breezes rather than industrial fans. Over 16 to 20 hours, moisture reduces by 60% - naturally. This extended withering preserves the delicate aromatic compounds that define Borokai's character.

06 Stage Six

Gentle Rolling

Using low-pressure brass rollers, the limp, withered leaves are gently twisted. This controlled bruising ruptures the leaf's cell membranes, releasing the natural juices and essential oils that coat the exterior - beginning the chain of chemistry that will eventually give the tea its body, colour, and malt.

07 Stage Seven

Natural Oxidation

The rolled leaves rest on cool stone tables in humidity-controlled rooms, absorbing oxygen slowly. As oxidation progresses, they turn from green to copper - and the Assam signature builds: bold, baked-malt, and the distinctive golden tips that define an authentic estate-quality leaf.

08 Stage Eight

Wax-Sealed Packaging

At the precise moment of full oxidation, the leaves are gently fired at low temperature to halt the process. They are hand-sorted by leaf grade, vacuum-packed into lined tin canisters, and each one is stamped with the Borokai hot wax seal - certifying the harvest lot, flush date, and plot number.

The Leaf & Sourcing

What makes single-estate tea superior to blended teas? +

Most commercial tea is blended from hundreds of estates to achieve an average, uniform profile. Borokai is strictly single-estate - every gram you brew comes from one plot of earth in Dwarbond. You taste the actual terroir: the specific minerals in the soil, the mist patterns, the altitude, the season. No averaging. No compromise.

What defines your 0% chemical certification? +

No synthetic pesticide, herbicide, or chemical fertilizer has touched Borokai soil in over a century. We rely entirely on biological inheritance - native forest bio-fertility, complex crop rotation, hand-weeding, and the natural ecosystem of wildlife that enriches our soil without any human-made intervention.

What is the difference between First Flush and Second Flush Assam? +

Our First Flush is the earliest spring harvest - the leaves are tender, the liquor is lighter with honeyed and floral notes. The Second Flush, harvested early summer, is the classic Assam expression: full-bodied, bold, and deeply malty with the famous coppery colour and golden tips.

Client Logistics

Do you ship internationally? +

How do I verify the authenticity of my Borokai canister? +

Every canister carries a hot wax estate seal stamped with the harvest date, plot location within Dwarbond, and an individual batch serial number. We also provide a printed provenance certificate inside each order from our private client allocations.

The Infusion Ritual

What is the ideal brewing method for Borokai teas? +

Use freshly drawn filtered water heated to 95°C (203°F). Measure 2 grams per 200ml cup. Steep 3–4 minutes for a balanced brew, up to 5 minutes for a bolder, malty expression perfect with a dash of milk. We recommend brewing in a warmed, ceramic or glass vessel to allow the leaves to open fully.

How should I store Borokai tea once opened? +

Store in the sealed tin canister away from direct light, moisture, and strong odors. Our vacuum-sealed packaging maintains freshness for 18–24 months unopened. Once opened, consume within 6 months for peak character - though it rarely lasts that long.
